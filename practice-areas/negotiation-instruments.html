Negotiation Instruments

Expert guidance on negotiable instruments and commercial paper

Overview

Negotiable instruments are specialized documents that promise payment of money, either on demand or at a specified future date. These financial instruments play a crucial role in business transactions and commerce. At Chetluru Srinivas & Associates, we provide comprehensive legal services related to all aspects of negotiable instruments.

Our team has extensive experience in drafting, reviewing, and litigating matters related to negotiable instruments, ensuring our clients' financial interests are protected in every transaction.

Types of Negotiable Instruments We Handle

  • Promissory Notes: Legal documents where one party promises to pay a specific sum to another party
  • Bills of Exchange: Written orders directing a third party to pay a specified sum
  • Cheques: Written orders to a bank to pay a specified amount from a specified account
  • Certificates of Deposit: Time deposits with banks that restrict withdrawals for a specified period
  • Banker's Acceptances: Time drafts that a firm's bank guarantees to pay
  • Treasury Bills: Short-term government securities

Common Legal Issues

Our practice regularly handles a variety of legal matters related to negotiable instruments, including:

  • Dishonored cheques and legal remedies under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act
  • Disputes regarding forged signatures or alterations
  • Issues related to holder in due course status
  • Enforcement of promissory notes and bills of exchange
  • Defense against wrongful dishonor claims
  • Recovery proceedings for dishonored instruments
  • Drafting and reviewing negotiable instruments to ensure legal compliance
